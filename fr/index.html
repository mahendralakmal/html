L’Algorithme

SportUnleash es un talent de sports et un algorithme d ‘identification des capacités physiques des enfants. Ceci utilise des méthodologies non invasives, recherches fondées sur preuves, auto-évaluation, données de mesures physiques de champions sportifs, tests d’attributs de mouvement physiques, afin de recueillir, corroborer, confirmer et valider les conclusions et générer un rapport scientifiquement valable.

Le Rapport

Ce rapport permet d’identifier le sport et la position idéale pour votre enfant, celle qui correspondra à votre enfant, pour lui permettra de réussir selon ses capacités physiques. En identifiant le don sportif de votre enfant bien à l’avance, lui permettra d’avancer et même parfaire ses habilités pour devenir un sportif de classe mondiale dès les années scolaires du primaire et secondaire.

En addition, ce rapport fournit un guide sur la meilleure façon d’investir vos finances, soit en choisissant le bon entraîneur, et les domaines qui nécessitent plus de développement. Les habilités physiques naturelles correspondent à des sports qui imitent et requièrent les mêmes capacités de votre enfant. Ce rapport vous indique également si votre enfant a un talent capable de représenter son école en compétition, s’il/elle est capable de réussir une performance de haut niveau, ou s’il/elle sera capable de représenter son pays un jour, si les renseignements ici fournis sur le Rapport SportUnleash sont suivis avec diligence et l’enfant reste engagé tout le long des années scolaires.

Bénéfices

  1. Performance plus accélérée

    Lorsque votre enfant compétera dans un/des sport(s) suggéré(s) par SportUnleash, votre enfant commencera bientôt à se dépasser, en réalisant de grand progrès et devenant un des meilleurs athlètes de son group d’âge dans ce sport.

  2. Développe une bonne attitude

    Participer dans un sport qui est correspondant aux habilités naturelles de l’enfant, va le motiver à réussir, et entraîner plus dur pour être perçu comme le meilleur du groupe. Moins de conférenciers parentaux devront le/la rendre motivé(e) ou même engagé(e).

  3. Évite la Déception

    Généralement, le talent s’identifie au cours des années d’adolescence. Malheureusement durant cette période, la plupart perdent intérêt dans le sport et l’entraînement après de nombreux échecs, et les quelques enfants désireux ont moins d’années scolaires pour parfaire leur compétence, ainsi, coupant court leur une possible carrière sportive. Le rapport SportUnleash vous offre un préavis afin d’éviter les déceptions habituelles.

  4. Les maintient engagés dans le Sport

    Les enfants s’habituent à l’idée qu’ils peuvent devenir des vainqueurs. Ils commencer à jouir des victoires qu’ils obtiennent de leur travail, qui a leur tour leur donne envie de travailler encore plus dur. D’un autre côté, ils commencent à réaliser les avantages du travail en équipe, et développent l’habilité de comprendre gagner ou perdre dans leur foulée. Ceci est un processus de construction de caractère qui a un effet très positif sur votre enfant.

  5. Maximise vos investissements

    Le rapport SportUnleash vous indique où mieux investir vos ressources pour que votre enfant puisse jouir, prospérer et réussir dans un sport.

Pas n.˚ 8
Une fois que vous ayez reçu le rapport, répondez au dernier message texto par le mot «YES».

Human anatomy is inherently linked to human function in a way that each cell, tissue, organ and system combine to create the myriad of human activity we call living.

The musculoskeletal system with its muscle cells, the muscle fibres, as its basic unit, which in turn form the larger muscle groups, together with the bones and joints make up the mechanism for human movement and sport. There is also the vital input from other systems such as the nervous system etc. The muscle fibres, depending on the genetic makeup of an individual may be fast twitch dominant (also known as the white fibre), slow twitch dominant (also known as the red fibre) or a similar mix of both.

Each sporting activity utilises these muscle fibres in a combination of ways. For example a one hundred metre sprinter will use the fast twitch fibres to generate massive power in their lower body muscles. On the other extreme a marathon runner will use the slow twitch red fibres to generate a sustained, longer period of muscle contraction and relaxation to last the length of the race.

If one were to identify what combination of these muscle fibres an individual has, then it is possible to predict what sort of sports activity that individual is suitable for. Further, focused training will enhance the growth of these fibres making the sportsperson perfectly adapted to the selected sporting activity.

The Game Has Changed

Today’s teenagers live in a fast paced, technologically driven world where everything is accessible at their finger tips. Smartphones, iPads, tablets and Facebook offer a plethora of entertainment making the need to get out and sweat uninviting. Today’s teenagers are more likely to pursue a sport only if they believe they have a good chance of succeeding and making it to their school team.

This kind of thinking is reaching epidemic levels whereby teenagers have started to lose out on the benefits sports offer. Many have difficulty working in a group, taking constructive criticism and even handling victory or defeat in their stride. When playing a sport these aspects are carefully nurtured in a controlled environment which helps to build strong character. The physical and mental health benefits lost out are too numerous to count for those children who give up doing a sport early in their school life. This loss tends to also affect their health in the long term as well.

It is not just about doing any sport, but choosing the appropriate sport for which each child is gifted with the appropriate physical attributes. New technology today allows us to match a child´s physical attributes with a particular set of sports. This knowledge will automatically help motivate and stimulate passion and commitment within children to continue in a sport throughout their school life and to become the best.

The Prize Money Bigger Than Ever

Over the last decade we have seen an exponential growth in the income generated by sports personalities and sports franchises the world over. As a result of this phenomenon, the desire and the possibility of becoming mega rich sportspeople have begun to drive parents and children alike to dream of such achievements and participate in a sport having such a target in mind; going to great lengths to see this become a reality.

However, neither the parents, nor the coaches have a dependable way of predicting the sport a child is best/better suited for. It is a random game of chance where fate is left in the “hands of the gods”. Therefore, the only way forward is for them to then pursue, invest time, energy and commit resources towards such a dream for years, in the blind hope that maybe their child will one day be the next prodigy of the nation or of a wealthy super successful sports franchise.

Emerging/Developing Nations Changing The Game

Sports high performance is the ability for an individual or team to compete in the world arena, and realise noteworthy achievements that are second to none. When such highly motivated and well-trained individuals compete for honours, the margin between victory and defeat is small. Such performances demand precision training and a pioneering spirit that re-invents and challenges the accepted norms for excellence.

Sadly however, whenever developing nations such as ours compete against champion teams from the developed world, the margin of defeat is enormous and, after decades of competing, we have become mere spectators at world events. As our economies have become wealthier however, we have invested in acquiring knowledge from coaches in the very countries that create world champions. Unfortunately, our teams’ and sportspeople’s real progress in comparison to the investments made annually is negligible.

Stunted By Lack Of Expertise

Expertise to be able to identify the talent of children at any age level is rare and when this does take place, it is only along the lines of selecting the best talent out of a pool of existing sportspeople in a particular sport or event. If any type of identification of talent is being carried out, it is taking place in a few developed countries behind closed doors and even in such instances by a handful of individuals working on an individual sportsperson at a time. No mechanism or expertise is widely available to the public to test the sports talent across schools or a nation within a short period of time, by analysing their physical capabilities and matching them with all the types of sports that exist.

This inability to advise, guide and promote the talent of sportspeople at early ages not only stunts our sports development programs, but also hinders our country’s capabilities to uplift its competitiveness in the world arena.
